    Prior, Likelihood, Posterior (Bayes Update)

    Bayes Update

    \[ \overbrace{p(\theta\mid\mathcal{D})}^{\text{posterior}} \propto \overbrace{p(\mathcal{D}\mid\theta)}^{\text{likelihood}} \overbrace{p(\theta)}^{\text{prior}} \]

    Likelihood reweights prior belief to produce posterior belief.

    Figure: posterior is the prior reshaped by observed evidence.

    Ref: Bayesian Data Analysis (3rd ed.) — Gelman, Carlin, Stern, Dunson, Vehtari, Rubin — CRC Press — 2013.

    Aleatoric vs Epistemic Uncertainty

    • Aleatoric: randomness in the data-generating process (noise, inherent variability).
    • Epistemic: uncertainty due to limited data or model mismatch (can shrink with better data/models).
    • This distinction matters because only epistemic can be reduced by more observations.
    • Many failures come from treating epistemic as aleatoric (or ignoring it).
    • Variance decomposition gives a practical split between the two effects.

    \[ \mathrm{Var}(Z)=\mathbb{E}[\mathrm{Var}(Z\mid\Theta)] + \mathrm{Var}(\mathbb{E}[Z\mid\Theta]) \]

    \[ \text{(interpretation: aleatoric)}\quad+\quad\text{(epistemic)} \]

    Ref: https://medium.com/data-science/

    The side-by-side comparison makes both uncertainty types visually concrete. Tell the audience that collecting more data mainly reduces epistemic uncertainty. Finally, we need to define what "trustworthy probabilities" mean: calibration.

    Three Entry Points of Uncertainty in Graph Dynamics

    • Structural uncertainty (\(A_{ij,t}\)): everyday example: we do not know all true contacts; edges are missed or misrecorded.
    • Parametric uncertainty (\(\theta\)): everyday example: transmission intensity differs across office, dormitory, and subway settings.
    • Observational uncertainty (\(\varepsilon_t, R_t\)): everyday example: delayed reports, content deletion, and changing reporting standards.
    • The same observations \(Y_t\) can often be explained by multiple \((A,\theta,X)\) combinations, so all three uncertainty types must be retained.
    • All three propagate to the same task output \(p(Q\mid\mathcal{D})\); otherwise risk intervals become overconfident.

    \[ A_{ij,t}\sim \mathrm{Bernoulli}(p_{ij,t}) \]

    \[ \theta \sim p(\theta\mid \mathcal{D}) \]

    \[ Y_t = H_t X_t + \varepsilon_t,\quad \varepsilon_t\sim \mathcal{N}(0,R_t) \]

    Ref: Discovering Latent Network Structure in Point Process Data — Linderman, Adams — ICML — 2014.

    Split uncertainty entry points into three categories to avoid labeling everything as generic noise. Each category affects the same \(Q\), but through different propagation paths. Next, we explain the core thesis: why uncertainty is not local in graphs.

    B: forecast spread size and timing under uncertainty.

    E: choose robust interventions that balance risk and cost.
